---
title: Building your lineup
description: A step-by-step guide to building an optimized Sorare lineup using SoGaffer.
---

SoGaffer walks you through the full lineup-building process — from inputting your cards to reviewing your final five before submitting to Sorare. Follow the steps below to get the most out of every gameweek.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start a new lineup session">
    Open SoGaffer and begin a new lineup build for the upcoming gameweek. SoGaffer loads the latest fixture data, player projections, and rarity information automatically.

    <Tip>
      Start your session at least 48 hours before the gameweek deadline. This gives you time to factor in late team news and injury updates before locking in your picks.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Input your available cards">
    Enter the player cards you have available to play. For each card, provide the player name, position, and rarity. SoGaffer matches each card to live player data and applies the correct rarity bonus multiplier.

    Make sure every card you are willing to play is included before reviewing recommendations — leaving out an eligible card may cause you to miss a better option.

    <Note>
      Verify that any cards already locked into another Sorare competition are excluded from your input. Playing the same card in two competitions simultaneously is not allowed on Sorare.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review player projections and recommendations">
    Once your cards are loaded, SoGaffer ranks your available players for each position based on:

    - Recent form and score history
    - Fixture difficulty for the upcoming gameweek
    - Double gameweek opportunities (players with two fixtures)
    - Rarity bonus applied to the projected score

    Review the recommended lineup and the projected scores. See [Analyzing players](/guides/analyzing-players) for a deeper look at how to interpret these metrics.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select your final five">
    Build your team by confirming one player for each required slot:

    | Slot | Position | Notes |
    |---|---|---|
    | Goalkeeper | GK | Clean sheet bonus is significant — prioritize good defensive fixtures |
    | Defender | DEF | Fixture difficulty matters more here than raw form |
    | Midfielder | MID | Look for high-volume players with assist and goal potential |
    | Forward | FWD | Prioritize attackers with favorable fixtures and recent scoring form |
    | Extra | Any outfield | Use this slot for your best double-gameweek player or highest-ceiling card |

    <Tip>
      Do not automatically default to your highest-rarity card for every slot. A Common card with a great fixture can outscore a Rare card facing a top-four defence. See [Gameweek strategy](/guides/gameweek-strategy) for more on balancing rarity vs. form.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review your lineup before submitting">
    Before finalizing, confirm:

    - All five players have a fixture this gameweek (no blanks)
    - Rarity bonuses are correctly applied per card
    - Any players with injury flags or uncertain availability are noted
    - Double gameweek players are in your lineup where possible

    If SoGaffer surfaces any warnings, read them carefully before proceeding.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit to Sorare">
    SoGaffer does not submit lineups directly to Sorare. Once you are satisfied with your selection, open your Sorare account and enter the same five players in the relevant competition. Double-check that each card you selected in SoGaffer matches the one you submit on Sorare — card rarity and edition affect your bonus multiplier.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common mistakes to avoid

<Warning>
  **Playing a card into a blank gameweek.** If a player has no fixture this gameweek, they score zero. Always confirm each player has a live fixture before submitting. SoGaffer flags blanks automatically.
</Warning>

<Warning>
  **Ignoring the extra slot.** Many players treat the extra slot as an afterthought. This slot often has the highest upside — use it for a player with a double gameweek or a standout fixture, not just your next-best remaining card.
</Warning>

<Warning>
  **Locking in your lineup too early.** Team news (injuries, suspensions, rotation) often breaks 24 hours before kick-off. Finalise your lineup as close to the deadline as practical.
</Warning>

<Warning>
  **Assuming rarity always wins.** A Unique card facing a strong opponent in poor form can underperform a well-placed Common card. Let the projections guide you, not rarity alone.
</Warning>
