Winning consistently in Sorare requires more than picking your best five cards — it requires a repeatable process each gameweek. This guide covers the strategic decisions that separate average managers from top performers.

## Pre-gameweek checklist

Work through these steps every gameweek before finalising your lineup.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check which leagues are active">
    Not all football leagues run on the same schedule. Open SoGaffer and navigate to **Fixtures** to see which leagues have matches this gameweek. Some gameweeks see major leagues take a break for international fixtures or cup competitions.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Identify blank and double gameweek players">
    Filter your card collection by **Blank** and **Double** using the fixture flags in the **My cards** panel. Know immediately which of your players are doubling up and which are sitting out. This shapes every decision that follows.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review injury and suspension news">
    Check the latest team news. SoGaffer surfaces injury flags pulled from available data, but official team news often comes 24–48 hours before kick-off. Cross-reference with the club's official channels or a reliable football news source before locking your lineup.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run SoGaffer's recommendations">
    With your cards loaded, generate a recommended lineup in SoGaffer. Review the projected scores and the reasoning behind each selection. You do not have to accept every recommendation — use it as a starting point, not a final answer.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check competing players in your pool">
    If two of your selected players face each other this gameweek, one will likely underperform. SoGaffer flags these conflicts. Consider swapping one player for an alternative who does not clash with your other selections.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Finalise your lineup close to the deadline">
    Set a reminder to check your lineup 1–2 hours before the gameweek deadline. Late team news — confirmed starting elevens, last-minute injuries — can change a selection entirely. Do not lock in 48 hours early if you can avoid it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Handling blank gameweeks

<Warning>
  A player with no fixture scores zero points. There is no exception and no partial credit. If your goalkeeper, defender, midfielder, or forward has a blank, you are effectively playing four players that week. Prioritise filling blanks before optimising any other variable.
</Warning>

<Warning>
  Be especially careful in the extra/flex slot during blank-heavy weeks. If you are already covering four mandatory positional slots with players who have fixtures, do not waste the extra slot on a blank — even if that card has a high rarity.
</Warning>

When key players in your collection have blanks:

- Check if you have an alternative card at the same position with a live fixture
- Consider using a Common card with a fixture over a Rare card with a blank — a real score always beats zero
- If you are short on coverage, this is a signal to diversify your card collection across more leagues and clubs

## Maximising double gameweek opportunities

Double gameweeks are the highest-leverage opportunity in Sorare. A player who performs at their average across two matches scores roughly twice their single-week output.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Stack doubles in the extra slot" icon="layer-group">
    The extra/flex slot has no positional restriction. Always try to fill it with a double gameweek player. If you have multiple DGW players, rank them by projected combined score and put the highest in the extra slot.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Prioritise DGW over rarity" icon="calendar">
    A Common card with a double gameweek will often outscore a Rare card with a single fixture, even after the rarity multiplier. Run the numbers in SoGaffer before defaulting to your highest-rarity card.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Check both DGW fixtures separately" icon="magnifying-glass">
    A double gameweek player with one easy and one very hard fixture is less valuable than one with two green fixtures. Use the Fixtures view in SoGaffer to assess each match individually.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Plan ahead for DGW weeks" icon="calendar-days">
    Fixture lists are often available several gameweeks in advance. If you spot a player with a strong double coming up, factor that into decisions you make now — even if their current gameweek is a single.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Balancing rarity bonuses vs. player form

Rarity multipliers reward you for owning higher-tier cards, but they do not override fundamentals. Use this framework:

- **Rarity wins** when projected scores are close and both players have similar fixtures
- **Form and fixture win** when the gap in projected performance is larger than the rarity multiplier can bridge
- **A blank always loses** regardless of rarity

For example: a Unique card projecting 40 points (×1.3 = 52) loses to a Common card projecting 60 points (×1.0 = 60). Do not let rarity bias override the numbers.

## Picking the extra/flex player

The extra slot accepts any position, making it the most flexible and highest-upside slot in your lineup. Use this priority order:

1. Best double gameweek player in your collection (any position)
2. Highest-ceiling single-gameweek player with a green fixture
3. Your strongest card by projected score after rarity bonus, if neither of the above applies

<Tip>
  Forwards and midfielders typically have higher score ceilings than goalkeepers and defenders. Unless your goalkeeper or defender has an exceptional double gameweek, the extra slot usually belongs to an attacking player.
</Tip>

## Late team news and injury management

Sorare locks lineups at the competition deadline, not at individual match kick-offs. This means a player confirmed in the starting eleven an hour before kick-off can still be added if the deadline has not passed — but also means you can get caught if a player is unexpectedly dropped after you submit.

To reduce risk:

- Avoid selecting players who are listed as **doubtful** or **50/50** unless no better alternative exists
- Check for rotation-risk players — managers who frequently rest key players in less important fixtures
- Keep an eye on pre-match press conferences, which typically happen the day before the match
- If a player you selected gets injured before kick-off but after the deadline, there is nothing you can do — this is an inherent Sorare risk to manage over the long run

<Note>
  SoGaffer surfaces available injury data, but official team sheets are only confirmed close to kick-off. Always treat injury flags in SoGaffer as a prompt to investigate further, not a definitive status.
</Note>
