SoGaffer surfaces several layers of player data to help you pick the right cards each gameweek. This guide explains how to read each analysis view and what to look for when comparing players at the same position.

## Player analysis views

SoGaffer provides three primary views for evaluating any player in your collection.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Form">
    The form view shows a player's scored points across their recent gameweeks. Use this to:

    - Identify players on a scoring run vs. players who have gone cold
    - Spot one-off outlier scores that inflate a player's average (for example, a hat-trick week)
    - Compare consistency — a player averaging 45 points every week is often safer than one swinging between 20 and 80

    SoGaffer indicates whether a player's trend is upward or downward based on their recent results.

    <Tip>
      For newly promoted clubs or players returning from injury, weight recent matches more heavily than a longer historical sample.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Fixtures">
    The fixtures view shows each player's upcoming matches, colour-coded by difficulty:

    | Colour | Meaning |
    |---|---|
    | Green | Favourable fixture (opponent ranked in the bottom third of the league) |
    | Amber | Neutral fixture |
    | Red | Difficult fixture (opponent ranked in the top third) |

    This view also flags:
    - **Double gameweeks (DGW):** Two fixtures in one gameweek — a major opportunity for double points
    - **Blank gameweeks (BGW):** No fixture — the player scores zero

    Plan two to three gameweeks ahead so you are not scrambling when a key player hits a blank.
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Score history">
    The score history view shows a rolling record of a player's Sorare scores. Use this to answer questions like: does this striker score consistently at home but go quiet away? Does this goalkeeper earn clean sheet bonuses against mid-table opposition but concede regularly against top-six sides?

    <Note>
      Score history reflects real Sorare scores based on the Sorare scoring algorithm applied to official match stats. These are not raw goals or assists — they include bonuses for decisive contributions, clean sheets, and other in-game actions.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Key metrics explained

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Average score">
    The player's mean Sorare score across their recent gameweeks. SoGaffer uses this as the base projection before applying fixture difficulty and rarity adjustments. A higher average does not always mean the right pick — check the upcoming fixture before committing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Fixture difficulty rating (FDR)">
    SoGaffer assigns each upcoming match a fixture difficulty rating. The rating is calculated from the opponent's recent defensive and attacking record, home/away advantage, and league position. A player's projected score is adjusted up or down based on their fixture difficulty — a striker with an easy fixture gets a meaningful projection boost.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Projected score">
    SoGaffer's estimate of what a player will score this gameweek. It combines the player's average score with the fixture difficulty adjustment. For double gameweeks, SoGaffer combines projected scores across both fixtures. Treat projections as a guide — football is inherently unpredictable.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Score ceiling and floor">
    Alongside the projected score, SoGaffer shows a ceiling (optimistic estimate) and floor (pessimistic estimate) based on score variance. High-ceiling players are useful in the extra slot where you want upside. High-floor players are safer for the mandatory positional slots where scoring zero is costly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Rarity bonus multiplier">
    Each card rarity applies a score multiplier in Sorare competitions. SoGaffer applies the appropriate multiplier based on the card rarity you input. The effective projected score shown already includes this rarity bonus.

    <Note>
      Exact multiplier values are set per competition by Sorare and can vary by season. Check the competition details page on Sorare for the specific multipliers that apply to each contest you are entering.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Minutes played">
    A player who regularly plays 90 minutes has more opportunities to score than a substitute who comes on at 70 minutes. SoGaffer shows average minutes played per match so you can filter out rotation risks — especially important for teams with squad depth.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Comparing players at the same position

When choosing between players at the same position, use this priority order:

1. Does either player have a blank gameweek? Eliminate blanks first.
2. Does either player have a double gameweek? A DGW player with decent form almost always wins.
3. Compare fixture difficulty — a green fixture beats a red one even if the player's average is slightly lower.
4. Compare form trend — prefer the player trending upward.
5. Apply rarity bonus — if projected scores are close, the higher-rarity card edges ahead.

## Identifying double gameweek players

Double gameweeks are the single biggest edge in Sorare. SoGaffer identifies DGW players in the fixture view. Their projected scores cover both matches, so their numbers will appear significantly higher than single-fixture players.

<Note>
  Not all leagues align their gameweeks at the same time. A player in the Premier League may have a double while your La Liga player has a single. Always check the fixture view for each player individually rather than assuming gameweek schedules match across leagues.
</Note>

## When to bench a high-rarity card

Benching a Rare or Super Rare card feels uncomfortable, but it is often the correct decision. Consider playing a lower-rarity card over a higher-rarity one when:

- Your high-rarity card has a blank gameweek and the lower-rarity card has a double
- Your high-rarity card faces a very difficult fixture and the lower-rarity card has an easy one
- Your high-rarity card's player is carrying an injury or has been dropping in minutes
- The projected score difference (after rarity multiplier) still favors the lower-rarity option

The rarity bonus is a multiplier on performance — it cannot overcome a zero from a blank or a poor score against a dominant opponent.
